About the Book

Honor, Service, and the Power of Hope

Arts & Photography / Museums & Collections

A powerful historical work honoring the lives, service, and sacrifice of Black and minority veterans of World War II. Through carefully preserved objects from the ACES Veterans Museum, the book connects personal artifacts to the larger story of the war and its lasting social impact.

It highlights how these veterans faced war abroad and discrimination at home — yet acted on hope, believing in a better future. More than a record of history, this is a tribute to courage, resilience, and the everyday objects that carry extraordinary stories.

About the Author

A.V. Hankins MD FACP

A medical doctor and preservation advocate, A.V. Hankins MD FACP brings both scholarly depth and heartfelt purpose to this work — devoting meaningful effort to honoring veterans and helping educate future generations.

“These veterans did more than serve — they acted on hope.”

As Founder and Director of the ACES Veterans Museum, A.V. Hankins MD FACP has played a vital role in preserving the spaces, histories, and personal stories of Black and minority veterans and their families. Through its collections, the museum serves not only as an educational resource, but as a lasting tribute to remembrance and legacy.
